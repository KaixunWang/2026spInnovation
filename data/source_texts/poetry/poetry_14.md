Perspective never withers from their eyes; They keep that docile edict of the Spring That blends March with August Antarctic skies: These are but cows that see no other thing Than grass and snow, and their own inner being Through the rich halo that they do not trouble Even to cast upon the seasons fleeting Though they should thin and die on last years stubble. And they are awkward, ponderous and uncoy . . . While we who press the cider mill, regarding them We, who with pledges taste the bright annoy Of friendships acid wine, retarding phlegm, Shifting reprisals (til who shall tell us when The jest is too sharp to be kindly?) boast Much of our store of faith in other men Who would, ourselves, stalk down the merriest ghost.
